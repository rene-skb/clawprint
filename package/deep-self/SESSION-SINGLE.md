# Deep Self Assessment — Single Session

*One conversation. ~25-35 minutes. Everything you need to calibrate.*

---

## Opening

> "We're going to have a conversation about how you think, what drives you, and where you do your best work. There are no right answers. I'm building a working model of you so I can actually be useful. Ready?"

---

## Questions

Each question scores across multiple frameworks. Follow-up prompts are suggestions, not a script. Follow the conversation where it goes.

### 1. When you have free time with nothing planned, what does your mind gravitate toward?

**Scores:** Openness · Holland Code
**Listen for:** Abstract vs. practical thinking. Creative exploration vs. routine comfort. What they're naturally drawn to reveals both curiosity level and work orientation.
**Follow-up:** "Is that typical or does it change depending on your mood?"

### 2. How do you feel about ideas that challenge your existing worldview?

**Scores:** Openness · Enneagram (Type 5 gets excited, Type 6 gets cautious, Type 1 evaluates against principles)
**Listen for:** Excitement about new perspectives vs. preference for what's proven. How they hold uncertainty.

### 3. Is your discipline consistent, or does it depend on how interested you are?

**Scores:** Conscientiousness · Enneagram motivation
**Listen for:** Engagement-dependent discipline is extremely common and important to identify. Reveals whether structure comes from within or from interest. Type 7s and 5s often have wildly variable discipline.
**Follow-up:** "What happens to the stuff that doesn't interest you?"

### 4. When you disagree with someone's approach, what's your instinct?

**Scores:** Agreeableness · Enneagram stress pattern · Attachment style
**Listen for:** Speak up vs. process privately. Confront vs. accommodate. Direct vs. diplomatic. How they handle friction tells you about conflict style, people-pleasing tendency, and assertiveness.
**Follow-up:** "Has that always been your pattern, or did you learn it?"

### 5. After a full day of socializing, how do you feel?

**Scores:** Extraversion
**Listen for:** Energized vs. drained. This is the cleanest introversion/extraversion signal.
**Follow-up:** "What's your ideal social setup — one-on-one, small group, or big gathering?"

### 6. Do you process thoughts by talking them out or thinking them through privately first?

**Scores:** Extraversion · Communication style calibration
**Listen for:** External vs. internal processing. Directly calibrates how verbose or concise your agent should be by default.

### 7. What's your deepest fear in how others might perceive you?

**Scores:** Enneagram core fear · Neuroticism
**Listen for:** This maps almost directly to Enneagram type. Type 3 fears being worthless. Type 5 fears being incompetent. Type 4 fears having no identity. Type 1 fears being corrupt. Also reveals anxiety patterns and self-consciousness level.
**Follow-up:** "How much does that fear actually drive your decisions day to day?"

### 8. When you're overwhelmed, what's your default coping mechanism?

**Scores:** Enneagram stress direction · Neuroticism
**Listen for:** Withdrawal (5, 4, 9), control (1, 8, 3), seeking support (2, 6, 7). Also reveals emotional resilience and recovery patterns.
**Follow-up:** "How long does it usually take you to bounce back?"

### 9. What does avoidance look like for you? What do you tend to put off?

**Scores:** Enneagram type · Neuroticism · Conscientiousness
**Listen for:** Type 5 avoids emotional demands. Type 7 avoids pain/boredom. Type 9 avoids conflict. The specific thing they avoid is often the clearest Enneagram signal in the whole assessment.
**Follow-up:** "What happens when you can't avoid it anymore?"

### 10. How do you feel about asking for help?

**Scores:** Enneagram · Agreeableness · Attachment style
**Listen for:** Independence vs. interdependence. Self-sufficiency as identity vs. practical choice. Type 5s and 8s often resist help. Type 2s resist receiving it.

### 11. What kind of work feels like play to you?

**Scores:** Holland Code · Throughline · Openness
**Listen for:** This often reveals their primary Holland letter instantly. The answer should feel effortless and specific. If they struggle, rephrase: "When do you completely lose track of time?"

### 12. Do you prefer building something yourself or orchestrating other people building it?

**Scores:** Holland Code · Extraversion · Enneagram
**Listen for:** Realistic/Artistic (build) vs. Enterprising (orchestrate) vs. Social (teach/guide). Also reveals control preferences and collaboration style.

### 13. What do people consistently come to you for — not your job title, but what they actually ask you to do?

**Scores:** Throughline · Holland Code · Extraversion
**Listen for:** External validation of throughline. What others see that they might not value in themselves. The gap between "what I'm asked to do" and "what I love doing" is what they're good at vs. what they're uniquely built for.

### 14. What's something you do easily that other people seem to struggle with?

**Scores:** Throughline
**Listen for:** Natural talent that's become invisible to them. Often the throughline is so natural they discount it. Push gently if they deflect.

### 15. What topic could you talk about for hours but rarely get to?

**Scores:** Openness · Throughline · Enneagram (unmet depth needs)
**Listen for:** Intellectual passion and unmet needs. Often reveals hidden depth, private interests, and where they feel most alive intellectually. The "rarely get to" part reveals social context and potential loneliness around certain interests.

### 16. What's the thread that connects your interests? If you zoom out on everything you're drawn to, is there a pattern?

**Scores:** Throughline (throughline) · Openness · Enneagram core motivation
**Listen for:** This is the money question. Their answer is often the Throughline stated in their own words. Give it space. If they can't see the thread, offer what you're noticing from the conversation so far and let them react.
**Follow-up:** "Does that thread show up in your work too, or just your personal interests?"

### 17. How important is it to you that the way something looks matches how well it works?

**Scores:** Holland Code (Artistic) · Conscientiousness
**Listen for:** Form vs. function orientation. High Artistic types can't separate the two. Practical types prioritize function. This calibrates how much your agent should care about polish and presentation.

### 18. Do you feel more motivated by moving toward something you want, or away from something you fear?

**Scores:** Enneagram · Neuroticism · Motivation calibration
**Listen for:** Approach vs. avoidance motivation. Critical for calibrating how your agent frames goals and nudges. Approach-motivated people respond to possibility. Avoidance-motivated people respond to risk of missing out or falling behind.

### 19. What does your inner critic sound like?

**Scores:** Neuroticism · Enneagram (Type 1 has a loud one, Type 9 barely hears it)
**Listen for:** Severity and frequency of self-criticism. The tone of the inner critic tells you how gentle or direct your agent should be with feedback.
**Follow-up:** "Is that voice useful or mostly noise?"

### 20. When someone close to you goes quiet, what's your first assumption?

**Scores:** Attachment style · Neuroticism · Agreeableness
**Listen for:** Secure ("they're busy"), anxious ("did I do something wrong"), avoidant ("good, space"). This calibrates how your agent should handle silence and check-ins.

## Synthesis (5-10 minutes)

After the questions, take a moment to compile your read. Then present it:

> "Here's what I'm seeing across everything we've talked about..."

### Present Each Framework

**Big Five:** Score each trait 1-15. Explain briefly why.
> "Openness: 12 out of 15. You're deeply curious, drawn to abstract ideas, and you follow rabbit holes. But you also have a practical streak that keeps you grounded."

**Enneagram:** Name the type and wing. Explain the core motivation and fear.
> "I'm reading you as a [Type]w[Wing]. That means you're driven by [motivation] and your core fear is [fear]. Under stress you tend to [pattern]. At your best, [growth pattern]."

**Holland Code:** Assign the 3-letter code.
> "Your Holland Code is [X][Y][Z]. That means you naturally thrive when you're [description of each letter]."

**Throughline:** Name it in one sentence.
> "Your throughline is [doing X] for [whom] because [unique quality]."

**Throughline:** Connect it all.
> "The thread running through everything: [2-3 sentences that tie the whole picture together]."

### Then ask:
> "Does that resonate? What would you adjust?"

Give them space. This is often an emotional moment. People don't hear their throughline named out loud very often. Let them sit with it, react, correct, and add nuance.

**Their reactions and corrections are the most valuable calibration data in the whole process.** Update your read based on what they say.
