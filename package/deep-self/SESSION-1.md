# Session 1: How You Think and Decide

*Focus: Big Five personality traits. ~20 minutes.*

---

## Opening

Start warm. This should feel like a conversation, not a test.

> "We're going to have a conversation about how you think and make decisions. There are no right answers — I'm just trying to understand how your mind works so I can work with you better. Ready?"

---

## Questions

Use these as starting points. Follow the conversation where it goes. You don't need to ask every question — follow the signal.

### Openness

1. **When you have free time with nothing planned, what does your mind gravitate toward?**
   - *Looking for:* Abstract thinking, creative exploration vs. practical tasks, routine comfort

2. **How do you feel about ideas that challenge your existing worldview?**
   - *Looking for:* Excitement about new perspectives vs. preference for what's proven

3. **Describe the last thing you went down a rabbit hole on.**
   - *Looking for:* Breadth and depth of curiosity, how far they follow an interest

4. **Do you tend to think in abstractions or concrete specifics?**
   - *Looking for:* Conceptual vs. practical orientation

### Conscientiousness

5. **How do you approach a big project with no deadline?**
   - *Looking for:* Self-imposed structure vs. drift, follow-through patterns

6. **When you make a commitment, how reliable are you in keeping it?**
   - *Looking for:* Honest self-assessment, not idealized version

7. **Is your discipline consistent or does it depend on how interested you are?**
   - *Looking for:* Engagement-dependent conscientiousness (very common, important to identify)

8. **How does your physical space usually look — organized, creative chaos, or somewhere between?**
   - *Looking for:* External manifestation of internal organization

### Extraversion

9. **After a full day of socializing, how do you feel?**
   - *Looking for:* Energized vs. drained. Classic introversion/extraversion signal.

10. **Do you process thoughts by talking them out or thinking them through privately first?**
    - *Looking for:* External vs. internal processing

11. **How do you feel about being the center of attention?**
    - *Looking for:* Comfort with visibility, social energy orientation

12. **What's your ideal social configuration — one-on-one, small group, or big gathering?**
    - *Looking for:* Social preference patterns

### Agreeableness

13. **When you disagree with someone's approach, what's your instinct — speak up immediately or process it privately first?**
    - *Looking for:* Conflict approach, directness vs. accommodation

14. **How important is it to you that people around you are comfortable?**
    - *Looking for:* People-pleasing tendency vs. comfort with discomfort

15. **Describe a time you said something difficult that needed to be said.**
    - *Looking for:* Capacity for difficult conversations, how they handle friction

16. **Do you tend to trust people's intentions or look for what they might be missing?**
    - *Looking for:* Default trust level, skepticism patterns

### Neuroticism

17. **What does your inner critic sound like?**
    - *Looking for:* Severity and frequency of self-criticism

18. **How do you handle uncertainty — like waiting for important news?**
    - *Looking for:* Anxiety patterns, tolerance for ambiguity

19. **When something goes wrong, how long does it take you to bounce back?**
    - *Looking for:* Emotional resilience, recovery speed

20. **What does stress look like for you? How would someone close to you know you're stressed?**
    - *Looking for:* Stress manifestation, self-awareness about emotional patterns

---

## Wrap Up Session 1

Reflect back what you're seeing:

> "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm noticing about how you think..."

Share your emerging read of their Big Five profile. Let them react, correct, and add nuance. This calibration loop is where the real signal lives.

**Don't score yet** — wait until all sessions are complete for the full picture.
