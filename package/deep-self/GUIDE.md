# Deep Self Assessment — Facilitator Guide

*Read this before starting the assessment. This is how you guide your human through the process.*

---

## What This Is

A conversational personality assessment that maps your human across four validated frameworks:

1. **Big Five (OCEAN)** — The gold standard in personality psychology. Five traits, each scored 1-15.
2. **Enneagram** — Core motivations and fear patterns. What drives them underneath.
3. **Holland Code** — Career/work alignment. Where they naturally thrive.
4. **Zone of Genius** — Gay Hendricks' framework. The intersection of talent, passion, and flow.

## Your Role

You're not a therapist. You're a curious, warm conversation partner who happens to be building a working model of this person. Think first date meets job interview meets late-night philosophy talk.

**Do:**
- Ask follow-up questions when answers feel surface-level
- Reflect back what you hear ("So it sounds like...")
- Notice patterns across answers and name them
- Share your emerging read — "I'm starting to see you as someone who..."
- Make it feel like a conversation, not a questionnaire

**Don't:**
- Rush through questions to "finish"
- Accept one-word answers without probing
- Diagnose or pathologize
- Make them feel judged for any answer
- Skip to scoring before you have enough signal

## Scoring

You're scoring Big Five traits conversationally, not with a formal instrument. Use the questions to build a picture, then assign scores based on the full conversation.

### Big Five Scoring Guide (1-15 scale)

**Openness to Experience**
- 1-5 (Low): Practical, conventional, prefers routine, concrete thinking
- 6-10 (Medium): Balanced between curiosity and pragmatism
- 11-15 (High): Imaginative, curious, loves abstract ideas, seeks novelty

**Conscientiousness**
- 1-5 (Low): Flexible, spontaneous, struggles with follow-through
- 6-10 (Medium): Organized when it matters, can be inconsistent
- 11-15 (High): Disciplined, detail-oriented, follows through reliably

**Extraversion**
- 1-5 (Low): Introspective, prefers small groups, recharges alone
- 6-10 (Medium): Comfortable in both social and solitary settings
- 11-15 (High): Energized by people, talkative, seeks stimulation

**Agreeableness**
- 1-5 (Low): Direct, competitive, challenges others, values truth over harmony
- 6-10 (Medium): Cooperative but can push back when needed
- 11-15 (High): Empathetic, conflict-averse, prioritizes others' feelings

**Neuroticism**
- 1-5 (Low): Emotionally stable, calm under pressure, resilient
- 6-10 (Medium): Normal emotional range, sometimes stressed
- 11-15 (High): Sensitive, anxious, experiences strong emotional swings

### Enneagram Types (Quick Reference)

| Type | Name | Core Motivation | Core Fear |
|------|------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | Reformer | Being good/right | Being corrupt |
| 2 | Helper | Being loved | Being unwanted |
| 3 | Achiever | Being valuable | Being worthless |
| 4 | Individualist | Being unique | Having no identity |
| 5 | Investigator | Being competent | Being helpless |
| 6 | Loyalist | Being secure | Being abandoned |
| 7 | Enthusiast | Being satisfied | Being deprived |
| 8 | Challenger | Being in control | Being harmed |
| 9 | Peacemaker | Being at peace | Conflict/loss |

Wings: Most people lean toward one adjacent type (e.g., 5w4 or 5w6).

### Holland Code (RIASEC)

| Code | Type | Thrives In |
|------|------|-----------|
| R | Realistic | Hands-on, practical, building things |
| I | Investigative | Research, analysis, understanding systems |
| A | Artistic | Creative expression, design, originality |
| S | Social | Teaching, helping, interpersonal work |
| E | Enterprising | Leading, persuading, starting things |
| C | Conventional | Organizing, data, structured processes |

Assign a 3-letter code (e.g., AIR = Artistic-Investigative-Realistic).

### Zone of Genius

Four zones from Gay Hendricks' *The Big Leap*:
- **Zone of Incompetence** — Things others do better. Stop doing these.
- **Zone of Competence** — Things you do adequately. Anyone could.
- **Zone of Excellence** — Things you do extremely well. Often mistaken for genius. Can become a trap.
- **Zone of Genius** — Where talent meets flow meets unique contribution. Time disappears. This is the goal.

Help them identify their Zone of Genius by looking for:
- What activities make time disappear?
- What do people consistently come to them for?
- What would they do even if nobody paid them?
- Where do talent, passion, and unique perspective intersect?

## Session Flow

### Default: Single Session (~25-35 minutes)
Read `SESSION-SINGLE.md` for the full question set. 20 questions designed to score across all four frameworks simultaneously. Most questions pull double or triple duty, so you cover the same ground as a longer assessment in one sitting.

This is the recommended approach. One conversation, no dropout risk, results by the end.

### Alternative: Three Sessions (~45-50 minutes total)
If your human prefers shorter sittings or wants to go deeper, split across three sessions:
- Session 1: How You Think and Decide (~20 min) — `SESSION-1.md`
- Session 2: What Drives You (~15 min) — `SESSION-2.md`
- Session 3: Where You Thrive (~15 min) — `SESSION-3.md`

### After the Assessment: Calibration
Read `CALIBRATION.md` to synthesize results into SOUL.md.

## Important Notes

- **Your human learns too.** This isn't just data collection for you. Many people discover things about themselves they hadn't articulated before. Let those moments breathe.
- **No wrong answers.** Every profile is valid. The goal is accuracy, not optimization.
- **Trust your read.** After 25-35 minutes of deep conversation, you'll have a strong intuitive sense of this person. Trust it, then validate it against the frameworks.
- **The calibration loop is everything.** When you present your read at the end, their reactions and corrections are the most valuable data in the whole process.
