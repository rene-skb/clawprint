# Session 2: What Drives You

*Focus: Enneagram type, core motivations, attachment patterns. ~15 minutes.*

---

## Opening

> "This session is about what drives you underneath — your motivations, fears, and patterns. Some of these questions go deeper than the first session. Take your time."

---

## Questions

### Core Motivation

1. **What's the thing you most want people to understand about you?**
   - *Looking for:* Core identity need. Type 4 wants uniqueness, Type 5 wants competence, Type 3 wants to be seen as valuable.

2. **What's your deepest fear in how others might perceive you?**
   - *Looking for:* Core fear pattern. Maps directly to Enneagram types.

3. **When you feel most like yourself, what are you doing?**
   - *Looking for:* Alignment between stated identity and actual behavior. Where do they feel most alive?

4. **What would you do if you knew nobody would judge you for it?**
   - *Looking for:* Suppressed desires, what social pressure holds back

### Stress and Growth

5. **When you're overwhelmed, what's your default coping mechanism?**
   - *Looking for:* Stress direction. Enneagram types move to specific types under stress.
   - Withdrawal? (5, 4, 9)
   - Control? (1, 8, 3)
   - Seeking support? (2, 6, 7)

6. **What does avoidance look like for you? What do you tend to put off?**
   - *Looking for:* Avoidance pattern. Each type avoids something specific:
   - Type 5 avoids emotional demands
   - Type 7 avoids pain/boredom
   - Type 9 avoids conflict

7. **When you're at your best, what's different about how you show up?**
   - *Looking for:* Growth direction. Contrast with stress response.

8. **What triggers you? Not annoyances — the things that really get under your skin.**
   - *Looking for:* Core sensitivity. Often maps directly to Enneagram fear.

### Relationships and Autonomy

9. **How do you feel about asking for help?**
   - *Looking for:* Independence vs. interdependence. Self-sufficiency patterns.

10. **What does your relationship to silence look like? Are you comfortable with long pauses?**
    - *Looking for:* Internal processing style, comfort with emptiness

11. **How much alignment do you need with someone to work well together?**
    - *Looking for:* Attachment patterns. High alignment need on identity stuff? Low on execution?

12. **When someone close to you goes quiet, what's your first assumption?**
    - *Looking for:* Attachment security, anxiety patterns in relationships

### Depth

13. **What's a belief you hold that most people in your life wouldn't expect?**
    - *Looking for:* Hidden depth, private intellectual life, gap between public and private self

14. **What topic could you talk about for hours but rarely get to?**
    - *Looking for:* Intellectual passion, unmet needs for depth

15. **Do you feel more motivated by moving toward something you want, or away from something you fear?**
    - *Looking for:* Approach vs. avoidance motivation. Critical for calibrating how your agent motivates them.

---

## Wrap Up Session 2

Share your emerging Enneagram read:

> "I'm seeing a pattern that looks like [Type X] — someone driven by [core motivation] who fears [core fear]. When you're stressed, you tend to [stress pattern]. When you're thriving, [growth pattern]. Does that resonate?"

Let them react. The moment they say "that's exactly right" or "no, it's more like..." — that's where the real calibration happens.
