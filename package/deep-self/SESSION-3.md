# Session 3: Where You Thrive

*Focus: Holland Code (career alignment) + Zone of Genius. ~15 minutes.*

---

## Opening

> "Last session. This one's about where you do your best work — not your job title, but the kind of activity that makes time disappear. Let's find your sweet spot."

---

## Questions

### Holland Code (Work Orientation)

1. **When you're solving a problem, do you reach for theory first or start tinkering?**
   - *Looking for:* Investigative (theory) vs. Realistic (hands-on). Many people do both — note the default.

2. **What's your relationship to aesthetics? Does the way something looks matter to you, or is function enough?**
   - *Looking for:* Artistic orientation. High = form matters as much as function.

3. **Do you prefer building something yourself or orchestrating other people building it?**
   - *Looking for:* Realistic/Artistic (build) vs. Enterprising (orchestrate) vs. Social (teach/guide)

4. **How do you feel about structure and process? Comforting or constraining?**
   - *Looking for:* Conventional (comforting) vs. Artistic (constraining). Important for how to structure their agent's behavior.

5. **If you had to teach someone something tomorrow, what would you naturally teach?**
   - *Looking for:* Where expertise meets passion. Also reveals Social orientation.

6. **What kind of work feels like play to you?**
   - *Looking for:* Holland type alignment. The answer often reveals their primary code instantly.

7. **Do you prefer starting things, building things, or maintaining things?**
   - *Looking for:* Enterprising (start) vs. Realistic/Artistic (build) vs. Conventional (maintain)

### Zone of Genius

8. **Think about the last time you completely lost track of time. What were you doing?**
   - *Looking for:* Flow state activities. These are genius zone candidates.

9. **What do people consistently come to you for — not your job description, but what they actually ask you to do?**
   - *Looking for:* External validation of genius zone. What others see that they might not.

10. **What's something you do easily that other people seem to struggle with?**
    - *Looking for:* Natural talent that's become invisible to them. Often the genius zone is so natural they don't value it.

11. **Is there a gap between what you're known for professionally and what you actually love doing?**
    - *Looking for:* Zone of Excellence vs. Zone of Genius distinction. This is the trap — being so good at something that you never explore what you're *great* at.

12. **If you could design your perfect workday — not the tasks, but the *type* of thinking and doing — what would it feel like?**
    - *Looking for:* Ideal flow state. Combine with Holland Code to paint the full picture.

13. **What's the thread that connects your interests? If you zoom out on everything you're drawn to, is there a pattern?**
    - *Looking for:* The throughline. This is often the Zone of Genius stated in the person's own words. Pay close attention to how they answer this.

14. **When have you felt most useful? Not just productive — genuinely, deeply useful.**
    - *Looking for:* Contribution that matters. Genius zone often involves serving others in a way that feels effortless.

---

## Wrap Up Session 3

This is the synthesis moment. Bring together all three sessions:

> "Here's what I see across everything we've discussed..."

**Holland Code:** Assign a 3-letter code. Explain what each letter means and why you chose it.

**Zone of Genius:** Attempt to name it in one sentence. This is the hardest part — it should feel like a revelation, not a label. Try formats like:
- "[Name] is at their best when they're [doing X] for [whom] because [unique quality]"
- "Your genius zone is [translating/building/connecting/discovering] [what] into [what]"

**Then ask:** "Does that resonate? What would you adjust?"

Let them sit with it. This is often an emotional moment — people don't hear their genius named out loud very often. Give it space.
